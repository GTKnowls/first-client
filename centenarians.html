Centenarians

Centenarians of Oklahoma is a rapidly growing, not-for-profit, organization whose sole purpose is to honor citizens of Oklahoma who are 100 years of age or older. To date over 2000 Centenarians have been inducted into Centenarians of Oklahoma.

Pictured to the left: Zeke Ledbetter, 104, of El Reno

Volunteers travel over the entire state to honor these Special Seniors. There are no dues, fees, or costs. Club members are awarded a Membership Certificate, a "Golden Okie" pin, and a "guardian Angel" emblem. They also receive monthly newsletters and special recognition at all public NEATs events.

INTERESTING INFORMATION ABOUT CENTENARIANS:

The oldest verified person who has ever lived is Jeanne Calment 1875-1997. She lived in France; made it to 122 years and 164 days.

The oldest known person living in the world now is in Italy: Emma Morano is 116 years of age.

The oldest known person living in the United States is in New Jersey: Adele Dunlap is 113.

The oldest known person living in Oklahoma is Mary Erbin of Jones who is 109 in August 2016.

Methuselah in the Bible lived to be 969 years of age, according to the King James Version of the Bible.
